WEB design

E-commerce platform-based design and development across eBay, Amazon, and BigCommerce — turning client briefs into trustworthy storefronts.

Role
Web design & development
Year
2017 — 2020
Type
Client work · Frooition
Platforms
eBay · Amazon · BigCommerce

The main focus of my work at Frooition was the design and development of e-commerce stores. Each project started with a client brief — an opportunity to understand their specific wants and needs, including layout, features, and visual references. A short research phase followed, building a picture of the company, where they sit in the market, and how their competitors project themselves through tone and visuals. That same shape applied across every platform I designed for: eBay, Amazon, and BigCommerce.

With the brief and my interpretation of it in mind, I worked through a fast-paced iterative process to “work up” the look and feel of each store — keeping the design decisions deliberate and fit for purpose. I think a designer’s style should be more malleable than a traditional artist’s: my own preferences come second to the project’s visual goal.

01 — eBay Bringing depth to listing pages

The video above is a good example of the level of customisation possible on the eBay platform. Bringing a more traditional website-homepage feel — with JavaScript-enabled features, sophisticated CSS, consistent visuals and a modern layout — helps create a professional, trustworthy shopping environment.

Once a design is complete it goes to the client for review. Their feedback feeds into an amended preview, and that back-and-forth continues until they’re satisfied the design meets their needs and they’re happy to sign it off.

02 — BigCommerce Full-store storefronts

BigCommerce projects gave me more architectural freedom — designing across a full storefront including category pages, product pages, content blocks, and the navigation that holds it all together.

03 — Amazon Brand storefronts

Amazon projects came with their own platform-specific constraints — but within them there was scope to build a consistent brand presence: storefront landing pages, A+ content, and the product detail enhancements that turn a generic listing into a brand experience.
